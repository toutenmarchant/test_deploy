About the Seminar

Human trafficking or more properly understood as modern-day slavery where the focus is upon the exploitation of a victim by a perpetrator is a world-wide phenomenon which exists in every country in the world. It has been estimated that there are over 27 million victims currently in the situation of modern-day slavery, including forced labour, sex trafficking and organ harvesting. All these various forms of human trafficking have the elements of exploitation of a victim by a perpetrator where the victim is unable to escape arising from the use of fraud, force or coercion.

In Malaysia, modern-day slavery is faced by many migrant workers, refugees, asylum seekers and foreign spouses totaling close to 5 million. The Malaysian Government on average rescues about 3 victims daily and thus far since the inception of the Anti-Trafficking in Persons Act in 2007 has rescued over 3,000 persons.

 

About the Speaker:

Daniel Lo joined as Special Officer for human rights with Senator Datuk Paul Low, Minister in the Prime Minister’s Office in November 2013. Datuk Paul Low is the Minister for Governance, Integrity and Human Rights appointed by the Prime Minister in 2013. His role as special officer is to advise the Minister and other related government ministries and agencies on human rights matters. In this role he also engages with civil society, statutory bodies, business groups and diplomatic missions to strengthen institutions to the realisation of human rights in Malaysia and the region. Before joining Datuk Paul Low, He was with the Coalition to Abolish Modern-day Slavery (CAMSA) as Country Manager from 2009 and owing to the Coalition’s focused efforts, was appointed as a NGO representative to both the Council for Anti-Trafficking in Persons of Malaysian and the Selangor Anti-Trafficking in Humans Task Force in 2011. He was also a partner and practicing solicitor in his law firm, Andrew Khoo & Daniel Lo from where he brings and contributes over 15 years of his legal practice knowledge in Malaysia. During that time, his direct intervention in many migrant worker cases involving employment, immigration and criminal issues has provided him with first hand experiences of many violations which migrant workers face in Malaysia.
